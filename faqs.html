FAQs

I want to Volunteer! How can I be a part of Communal Health Support Scheme?

You can become a part of our community by Registering on www.chsscheme.org/volunteers and we will be in touch.

What is Communal Health Support Scheme?

Communal Health Support Scheme is a youth-led organization that provides health services and medical relief to vulnerable and underprivileged communities in Nigeria.

What benefits can I gain from volunteering with this organization?

As a Volunteer at Communal Health Support Scheme, you are able to impact underprivileged communities with healthcare development and get recognition for it.

Do I have to pay monthly fees?

No. However, you are required to contribute towards medical outreaches.

How often will I have to volunteer, is there a time commitment?

At Communal Health Support Scheme, medical outreaches are done quarterly, therefore you are required to be present at each outreach. However, some certain roles require a daily or weekly commitment.

Am I required to dress a certain kind of way as a Volunteer?

No. All dress codes for each outreach will be communicated prior to the outreach. Or a volunteer kit would suffice.

Will I get paid from deploying my professional skills?

All professional skills provided as a volunteer to Communal Health Support Scheme are unpaid unless outsourced from outside the volunteer community.

What happens if i get hurt during an outreach?

Every outreach has a medical team present who take responsibility to ensure every volunteer is sorted in the event of an accident.

Do I have to be a medical doctor or personnel to volunteer?

No. Non-medical professionals can provide their skills as there are other teams that ensure a successful medical outreach outside the medical team.

I do not have time to physically volunteer, how else can I help and contribute my quota?

Not to worry, Communal Health Support Scheme requires a lot of back-office development before successful medical outreaches are accomplished. You can provide your skills and expertise remotely where necessary. We also run awareness and advocacy campaigns for you to be a part of.
